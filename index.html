History of Singapore

1826

During the 19th century, the British empire was eyeing a port of call in this region to base its merchant fleet, and to forestall any advance made by the Dutch. Singapore, already an up-and-coming trading post along the Malacca Straits, seemed ideal....

1942

From 1942 - 1965, the British had abandoned Singapore and surrended to Japan, causing the Japanese Occupation to take place in Singapore.

1965

From 1963 - 1965, Malaya, Singapore, North Borneo and Sarawak were merged and Malaysia was formed. However, this merger does not last long as racial tension in Singapore increases due to the discriminatory policies imposed by the malays while Lee Kuan Yew and other political leaders prefer a fairer treatment among all races.

Present

From 1965 - 1990, Lee Kuan Yew grew and developed Singapore from a developing country to a well developed one by communicating and building relations with other countries.

2003

SARS was a deadly virus that orignated from Guangzhou. It affected Singapore from March to July.
