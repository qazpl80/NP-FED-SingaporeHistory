Introduction/Origin of SARS

The origin of SARS took place in 2002 where a then 45-year old man from Guangdong fell ill with fever and displayed respiratory symptoms in November 2002. He then infected four relatives but was not identified as the first SARS Patient until much later.

How it came to Singapore

First case happened in Singapore on March 1 2003. A woman who had been to Hong Kong fell ill and was admitted to Tan Tock Seng Hospital along with two other women. On March 15 2003, the disease has been coined as SARS. Singapore has 16 cases at the time.

Timeline of the incidents

On March 25 2003 Singapore experienced its first death from SARS. The father of the first case. A day later, a pastor who visited the first patient becomes the second person to die from SARS. Schools were then closed from March 27 to April 6. People who died from SARS must also be cremated within 24 hours. Temperature checks were also introduced for passengers entering from Singapore through Changi Airport. SGH is then hit by the outbreak and closure of schools is extended. Schools were reopened from April 9 to 16. The last patient was detected on May 18 2003 and the Singapore was declared SARS free on May 30. SARS was completely eradicated in SIngapore on July 16 2003.

Aftermath

There were repercussions after the incident. There were economies affected, particularly tourism as visitors number plunged as a result of SARS.
