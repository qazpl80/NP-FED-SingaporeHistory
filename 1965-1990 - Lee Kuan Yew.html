Lee Kuan Yew's Contributions

Improvement To The Economy

Despite the failure of the merger,Lee Kuan Yew continued to make clear decisions to improve Singapore's current status, his most important task was to fix the high unemployment of rate of Singapore. After several attempts of providing jobs for the public, he was finally able to increase the economy by attracting foreign investments from multinational corporations. By the 1970s, Singapore had become a major electronics exporter with the help of multinational corporations such as Texas Instruments & Hewlett-Packard setting their foundations in Singapore.

Tackling The Security of The Nation

Singapore's vulnerability was felt throughout the nation, with threats from the communists & Indonesia about their combative capabilities as a nation. Lee quickly sought for international recognition of Singapore's Isdependence as Singapore had gained admission to the United Nations. He requested help from Israel & Taiwan for advice on training & appointed Gok Keng Swee. In 1967, Lee introduced the concept of National Service where all able-bodied male Singaporean citizens age 18 & above have to serve National Service either in the Singapore Armed Forces, Singapore Police Force or the Singapore Civil Defense. By 1971, Singapore had 17 national service battalions (16,000 men) with 14 battalions (11,000 men) in the reserves.

Corruption in Singapore

Lee introduced legislation giving the Corrupt Practices Investigation Bureau (CPIB) greater power to conduct arrests, search, call up witnesses & investigate bank accounts & income-tax returns of suspected persons and their families. On 21 November 1986, He received a complaint of corruption against Teh Cheang Wan who was Minister for National Development at the time. As Lee was against corruption, he allowed the CPIB to carry out investigations on Teh but Teh committed suicide before he could face the consequences of his actions.
